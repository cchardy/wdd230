White Space and Clean Lines

Architectual Digest

www.architecturaldigest.com

Architectual Digest is a great example of clean lines with amble white space. White space can also be referred to as negative space or what remains "empty". It gives the eye a place to rest as well as creating a heirarchy for your attention.

Repetition

Better Homes and Gardens

www.bng.com

In the Better Homes and Gardens, the designer uses a few examples of repetition. The bright pink found in the title is also repeated in the sub-categories thus giving the page continuity.

Alignment

Elle Decor

www.elledecor.com

Alignment is about the positioning of the elements on a web page. This can be images, text, white space, etc. One rule that has stood out to me is that an element has to line up with at least one other element. In this example from Elle Decor, the title is adjusted to line up on the left and the right through kerning. The next hierarchy of text is center aligned but then the sub-categories are left-aligned. Of course the image frames are the same size and in perfect alignment. Another great aspect is that having varing alignments does add interest but if over done, the page will not look cohesive.
